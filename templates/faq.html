Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. What is Automated Online Exam Proctoring?

Automated Online Exam Proctoring is a system that monitors students during online exams using AI and webcam technology. It ensures exam integrity by detecting suspicious activities like face mismatches, multiple faces, or prohibited objects.

2. How does the face recognition feature work?

During the exam, the system captures your face via the webcam and compares it with the verified image you provided during registration. If a mismatch is detected, a violation is logged, and the instructor is notified.

3. What happens if I lose internet connection during the exam?

If you lose internet connection, the system will pause the exam timer. Once your connection is restored, you can resume the exam from where you left off. However, prolonged disconnection may result in exam termination.

4. Can I use a calculator during the exam?

Yes, you can use the built-in calculator provided in the exam interface. However, using an external calculator or any other unauthorized device is prohibited and may result in a violation.

5. What happens if I violate exam rules?

If a violation is detected (e.g., face mismatch, prohibited objects, or tab switching), the system will log the incident and notify the instructor. Repeated violations may result in exam disqualification.

6. How is my data protected?

Your data is securely stored and encrypted. We comply with data protection regulations to ensure your privacy. Your webcam footage and exam data are only accessible to authorized personnel.

7. Can I take the exam on a mobile device?

No, the exam must be taken on a desktop or laptop with a stable internet connection and a functioning webcam. Mobile devices are not supported.

8. What should I do if I encounter technical issues?

If you encounter technical issues, contact your instructor or the support team immediately. Provide details of the issue, including screenshots or error messages, for faster resolution.
